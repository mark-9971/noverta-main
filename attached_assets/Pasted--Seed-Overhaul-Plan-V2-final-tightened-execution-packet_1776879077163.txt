## Required design principles

### Wedge-first principles
- seed for the real product first
- derived wedge numbers must follow from primitive facts
- trust math must use the actual calendar/minute engine
- shared handling state must be plausible, not detached from underlying issues
- reports and exports must derive from the same seeded facts as the UI

### Demo-soon principles
- realistic district data is not enough; the demo must still work reliably
- first-login must expose 3–5 high-signal wedge examples without searching
- there should be more than 5 showcase cases; choose the best count and justify it
- role-switching must feel alive for admin, provider, case-manager, and para
- some surfaces should remain lightly populated if that is the honest maturity state
- demo-safe determinism matters: the district can be realistic without making demo moments dependent on luck

### No-cheating rules
- no backfills
- no post-seed edits to force compliance distribution
- no hard-setting KPI cards
- no manually patching compensatory totals, risk counts, alert counts, or trend lines after generation
- no mutating already-generated sessions into a different truth purely to hit targets
- no alert rows disconnected from underlying seeded facts unless the product truly stores them as primary operational objects
- no invisible “fix-up” pass that rewrites the district after simulation to make the demo prettier

### Directly seeded vs simulated vs derived vs sparse
Be explicit about:
- what should be directly seeded
- what must be emitted by historical simulation
- what must be derived by live product logic
- what can be lightly/sparsely seeded
- what should stay out of scope
