A. Strategic Interpretation
1. CORE WEDGE — What must work perfectly to win the pilot

The only question MetroWest needs answered daily: "Are we legally covered?" The core loop is Know → Why → Act → Prove:

Know which students are behind on mandated minutes (Dashboard, Compliance page)
Why (missed session logs, Action Center alerts)
Act (schedule makeup sessions, log sessions fast)
Prove (compliance report, IEP timeline, audit trail)
Truly core pages: Dashboard, Action Center, Compliance, Student Detail, Sessions/Quick-log, Alerts. Everything else is supporting or post-pilot.

2. BIGGEST PRODUCT RISKS

Compliance ↔ Scheduling is disconnected. The Scheduling Hub has no panel showing "Student X needs 45 more minutes this month — here's what's scheduled vs. what's needed." Admin has to open two different pages to answer one question.
Nav cognitive overload. 40+ visible items across ABA, Billing, Agencies, State Reports, Restraint/Seclusion, Guardian Portal, Student Portal. In a demo, this looks complex. A MetroWest coordinator should see 8 items, not 40.
Session quick-log is good but not surfaced from compliance context. There's no "Log a session now" shortcut from the student's compliance alert.
Too much is unfinished-looking for a demo. ABA Hub, Medicaid Billing, and State Reports are pilot distractions that make the product feel beta.
3. BEST MONETIZATION STORY

Feature	Tier
Dashboard, Action Center, Compliance, Alerts, Session logging, basic IEP	CORE
Scheduling Hub + compliance overlay, Advanced reports, Compensatory tracker, ABA data	PRO
Executive Dashboard, District Overview, Multi-school, Medicaid Billing, Agencies, SSO	ELITE
Never gate: Session quick-log, basic compliance alerts, the Compliance page itself. These are survival features — gating them kills the value prop.

4. DANGEROUS DISTRACTIONS — do not touch for pilot

ABA Hub (complex, not MetroWest priority), Restraint & Seclusion (niche + legal complexity), Medicaid Billing (state credentialing required), Student Portal, Guardian Portal, State Reports, Agencies. Leave them in the nav but collapsed; spend zero engineering time on them.

E. First Build Batch — Executing Now
Highest-value first move: "Minutes at Risk" panel in Scheduling Hub.

This is the single most impactful gap in the entire product. Every other compliance feature exists — but when a scheduler opens the Scheduling Hub to build a session plan, they have no idea which students need priority scheduling. Adding a "Minutes at Risk" tab to the Scheduling Hub closes the most important demo workflow: compliance alert → scheduling decision → session logged
