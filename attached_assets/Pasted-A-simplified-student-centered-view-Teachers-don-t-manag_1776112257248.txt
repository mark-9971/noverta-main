A simplified, student-centered view — Teachers don't manage caseloads like a BCBA. They need to see "my classroom" and which students have IEP accommodations/services that affect their instruction.
Accommodation checklists — "Student X needs preferential seating, extended time, and visual schedules." The IEP accommodations table exists in the database, but there's no teacher-facing view that surfaces these as actionable checklists.
Quick behavior logging — Not the full ABA data collection interface, but a simple way to note incidents ("John had 3 outbursts today") that feeds into the BCBA's data.
Schedule awareness — "When is this student being pulled for speech therapy?" so the teacher can plan around it. The schedule exists but isn't presented from a classroom teacher's perspective.
Progress note contribution — Teachers often contribute observations to IEP progress reports. There's no mechanism for a teacher to submit input that gets rolled into a formal report.
5. Paraprofessional (Para)
Paras are the front-line data collectors and most time-constrained users. They need:

A mobile-first, minimal data entry interface — The current session form and program data collection pages are desktop-oriented with many fields. A para standing next to a student needs big buttons, tap-to-record, and minimal typing.
"My schedule today" view — Not a full weekly calendar, but "Who am I with right now, and what am I supposed to be working on?" A simple daily agenda.
Quick-start session timer — Tap to start, tap to stop, session logged. The current session form requires selecting student, service type, dates, etc. before logging.
Guided data collection — When working on a program, the para needs step-by-step prompts: "Present stimulus → Record response → Record prompt level." The Program Builder wizard creates programs, but the live collection interface needs to be simpler and more guided.
Offline capability — Paras often work in gyms, cafeterias, or outdoor areas with poor connectivity. There's no offline support.
Read-only IEP goal summaries — Paras need to see what they're working on and why, but shouldn't be editing IEP goals or clinical programs.
