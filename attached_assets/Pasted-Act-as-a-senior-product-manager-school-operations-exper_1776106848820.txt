Act as a senior product manager, school operations expert, special education systems designer, BCBA-informed workflow reviewer, QA lead, and software architect.

You are reviewing an app for a school administrator.

This app is intended to manage and analyze:
- all student data tracking
- academic data
- behavioral data
- IEP goal tracking
- ABA tutor / paraprofessional / related service support
- protective measures / student safety planning
- service minutes tracking
- support staff scheduling based on student need

Your job is to critique the app and identify everything that is missing, weak, incomplete, risky, confusing, or not yet ready for real-world use in a school setting.

Context:
This is not a generic school app. It is meant for a school / special education / ABA-informed environment where staff need to track services, student needs, progress, compliance, and day-to-day support logistics.

Review the app through these lenses:

1. Product completeness
Assess whether the app fully supports the real workflows a school administrator would need.
Look for missing modules, weak workflows, and partial implementations.

Questions to answer:
- What major features are missing for a strong v1?
- What school admin tasks are not actually supported end to end?
- What workflows would break in real use?
- What data is being tracked insufficiently?
- What parts of the app seem too shallow for real school operations?

2. Student records and data tracking
Review whether the app properly supports:
- student profiles
- grade / classroom / caseload assignment
- academic performance tracking
- behavioral incident tracking
- ABC data
- frequency / duration / intensity tracking
- intervention tracking
- notes / observations
- trends over time
- attachments / documents
- filtering by student, classroom, staff, goal, date range, and program

Identify what is missing, too simplistic, or poorly structured.

3. IEP and goal tracking
Review whether the app properly supports:
- IEP goals and objectives
- baseline data
- progress monitoring
- mastery criteria
- reporting periods
- evidence collection
- progress summaries
- service delivery tied to goals
- goal ownership by staff or discipline
- alerts for goals lacking data

Identify missing features, missing validations, and likely compliance gaps.

4. Minutes and service tracking
Review whether the app properly supports:
- mandated minutes by service type
- delivered vs scheduled minutes
- monthly and weekly rollups
- missed minutes
- makeup minutes
- service logs
- discrepancies between required and delivered services
- minute tracking by student and by provider
- exportable reports for compliance review

Identify what is missing for a truly useful compliance-ready system.

5. Support staff scheduling
Review whether the app properly supports:
- assigning paras / ABA tutors / related service staff to students
- scheduling by time blocks
- prioritizing based on student need
- handling staff availability
- avoiding double-booking
- substitutions / absences
- classroom coverage
- recurring schedules
- last-minute changes
- balancing staff workloads
- matching staff skillset to student needs
- flagging uncovered supports

Identify what scheduling capabilities are missing or unrealistic.

6. Protective measures and student safety planning
Review whether the app properly supports:
- protective measures plans
- medical / allergy / elopement / aggression / toileting / communication-related supports
- supervision levels
- transportation supports
- crisis response instructions
- de-escalation protocols
- staff alerts and acknowledgments
- version history of plans
- restricted access for sensitive student information
- emergency quick-view summaries

Identify what is missing, unsafe, or too weak for real-world school use.

7. User roles and permissions
Review whether the app properly distinguishes between:
- school administrators
- BCBAs
- teachers
- paraprofessionals / ABA tutors
- related service providers
- support staff
- parents or guardians if applicable

Identify missing permissions, visibility rules, approval flows, or audit history.
Call out where sensitive student data could be exposed improperly.

8. UX / UI critique
Review the app for actual day-to-day usability in a busy school setting.

Questions to answer:
- What would be confusing or too slow for staff?
- What screens are missing?
- What tables, dashboards, filters, alerts, quick actions, or mobile-friendly views are missing?
- What would create duplicate work?
- What would frustrate admins, teachers, paras, or clinicians?

9. Data model / business logic
Review whether the app’s underlying logic appears complete.

Look for missing or weak handling of:
- student-to-staff relationships
- service mandates
- scheduling conflicts
- goal-to-data linkage
- compliance calculations
- archival / inactive students
- school year rollover
- attendance interactions
- missed session logic
- data validation
- duplicate entries
- change logs
- required fields
- exceptions and edge cases

10. Reliability / bugs / QA
Identify likely bugs and fragile areas, especially around:
- minute totals
- schedule conflicts
- duplicate logging
- stale data
- filtering problems
- incorrect rollups
- date logic
- permissions bugs
- race conditions
- partial saves
- deleted or inactive users/students breaking historical records

11. Security / privacy / compliance
Review the app as if it contains real student information.

Call out issues involving:
- FERPA/privacy concerns
- role-based access
- audit logs
- sensitive note visibility
- security of student behavioral / medical / IEP information
- session handling
- export controls
- data retention
- backup / recovery needs

12. Launch readiness
Determine whether this app is actually ready for school use.

Identify what is still needed before launch, including:
- onboarding
- settings
- admin controls
- report exports
- parent-safe documentation if relevant
- print-friendly views
- staff training aids
- error handling
- help text
- analytics
- notifications / reminders

Important instructions:
- Do not just praise the app
- Be critical, specific, and practical
- Assume this app will be used in a real school with real compliance pressure
- Point out anything that is missing, risky, underbuilt, or likely to fail in daily operations
- Call out features that look complete on the surface but are actually too shallow for real use
- Suggest concrete fixes, not vague advice

Return your answer in this exact structure:

1. Overall assessment
Briefly describe the app’s maturity level and whether it feels like an early prototype, decent v1, or something closer to production.

2. Findings table
Create a table with these columns:
- Issue / Missing Feature
- Area
- Why It Matters
- Severity (Critical / High / Medium / Low)
- Recommended Fix

3. Top missing features
List the 15 most important missing features to build next.

4. Top likely bugs / fragile points
List the 10 areas most likely to break or produce incorrect data.

5. Top UX improvements
List the 10 highest-impact interface or workflow improvements.

6. Top technical / architecture improvements
List the 10 most important backend / database / system design improvements.

7. Compliance / privacy / permissions risks
List the biggest risks related to school data, sensitive student info, and role access.

8. Launch blockers
State what absolutely must be fixed before real school staff should rely on this app.

9. Prioritized roadmap
Break recommendations into:
- Must fix before launch
- Build soon after launch
- Nice to have later

Be brutally honest and think like someone responsible for student safety, compliance, staffing, and operational reliability.
