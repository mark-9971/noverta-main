1. PRODUCT TRUTH YOU MUST OPTIMIZE AROUND
==================================================

This app does NOT have one universal center of gravity.

It has different primary workflows by role:

A. ADMIN / COORDINATOR / CASE MANAGER
These users are COMPLIANCE-FIRST.
Their core wedge is:
- service minute tracking
- compliance risk detection
- missed services / missed minutes
- compensatory exposure
- audit-ready reports / exports
- district / school oversight

Their main question is:
“Who is behind, where is the risk, what needs action, and what can I report upward?”

B. BCBA
These users are STUDENT-CENTERED with deeper clinical / behavior access.
Their core workflow is:
- my students / caseload
- sessions
- documentation / notes
- progress
- behavior / ABA tools
- supervision / deeper analysis where appropriate

Their main question is:
“Which students am I responsible for, what do they need, and what clinical work should I do next?”

C. SLP / OT / PT / GENERAL SERVICE PROVIDER
These users are also STUDENT-CENTERED, but they generally need less behavior-analysis depth than BCBAs.
Their core workflow is:
- my students
- my schedule
- session logging
- service delivery
- goals / notes / progress

Their main question is:
“Which students am I seeing, what services am I delivering, and what do I need to document?”

D. PARA / DIRECT STAFF
These users need the MOST SIMPLIFIED, TASK-ORIENTED experience in the app.
Their workflow should be:
- see today’s assignments
- see assigned students
- know what to do
- quickly confirm/log work
- access only the student context and supports relevant to their assignment

Their main question is:
“Where do I go, who am I assigned to, what should I do, and how do I quickly record it?”

Paras / direct staff should NOT be exposed to:
- district-wide compliance dashboards
- executive summaries
- advanced reports
- staff-wide comparisons
- advanced ABA analysis
- higher-level supervision tools
- broad student analytics outside their assignment
- admin/import/data-health/system tooling

2. CORE DESIGN RULES
==================================================

Use these rules as hard constraints:

1. Do NOT design one giant universal nav for all roles.
2. The app must have ROLE-BASED SHELLS, not just one shared sidebar with a few hidden links.
3. Admin experience should be COMPLIANCE-FIRST.
4. BCBA / provider / para experience should be STUDENT-FIRST.
5. The same student route/workspace can be shared, but DEFAULT TAB, WIDGETS, ACTIONS, and visible TABS should differ by role.
6. Tabs belong INSIDE workspaces/pages, not in the global sidebar.
7. Paras / direct staff should have the SMALLEST and SIMPLEST shell in the product.
8. BCBAs can have deeper analysis and clinical tools than paras or general service providers.
9. Reports, minute tracking, and compliance must remain highly prominent for admin roles because that is the core differentiator of the product.
10. Prefer lower-risk cleanup and consolidation over unnecessary rewrites.
