I want a thorough, end-to-end audit of the entire app navigation and workflow architecture.

Your job is NOT just to describe pages. Your job is to critically evaluate whether the product is clean, simple, efficient, trustworthy, and easy to use in real life.

Audit EVERY visible nav section and subpage, including:
- Overview
- Compliance and Risk
- IEP and Services
- ABA and Behavior
- Scheduling
- Financial and Executive
- Admin and Tools

Your goal:
Make sure everything works, every workflow makes sense, and the platform feels clean, straightforward, efficient, and not messy. I want the experience to feel tight, logical, and easy to use repeatedly for real users.

What I want you to do:

1. FULL NAV INVENTORY
- List every nav item and sub-item currently visible.
- Group them by section.
- Note duplicates, overlaps, confusing labels, dead-end pages, circular flows, or pages that feel out of place.

2. PAGE-BY-PAGE AUDIT
For every page:
- What is this page supposed to do?
- Is it functional right now?
- Does it load cleanly?
- Are there errors, broken states, empty junk states, fake/demo-only content, or misleading UI?
- Does the page earn its existence, or should it be merged, simplified, hidden, or cut?
- Is the name of the page clear and intuitive?
- Is the information density too high, too low, or about right?
- Does the page feel trustworthy enough for a real district / school / ABA workflow?

3. WORKFLOW AUDIT
Audit the major workflows across the product, not just individual pages.
For each major workflow, identify:
- Best entry point
- Expected user path
- Friction points
- Unnecessary clicks
- Duplicate actions
- Confusing loops
- Places where users have to jump between too many pages
- Missing shortcuts, summaries, next actions, or cross-links

Important workflows to audit:
- Admin / coordinator daily workflow
- Case manager workflow
- BCBA workflow
- Provider / therapist workflow
- Compliance risk review workflow
- IEP service minute tracking workflow
- ABA behavior documentation workflow
- Scheduling / make-up minutes / service planning workflow
- Executive oversight workflow
- Setup / onboarding / district administration workflow

4. CLEANLINESS / SIMPLICITY REVIEW
Judge the product against this standard:
- clean
- simple
- straightforward
- easy to learn
- easy to repeat daily
- low cognitive overload
- minimal clutter
- obvious next steps
- no messy duplication

For every section, tell me:
- What feels clean
- What feels cluttered
- What feels redundant
- What feels too complicated
- What feels out of order
- What feels bolted on
- What should be hidden from pilot users
- What should be combined or renamed

5. SECTION-BY-SECTION VERDICT
For each nav section:
- Is the section well organized?
- Are the pages in the right place?
- Are there pages that should move elsewhere?
- Are there pages that should be merged?
- Are there pages that should be removed from nav and accessed contextually instead?
- Does the section support a real user’s mental model?
- Does the section feel too broad, too fragmented, or just right?

6. FIND ALL MESS
I specifically want you to hunt for:
- circular navigation
- duplicated concepts in multiple places
- pages that say similar things with slightly different names
- features that look important but are not actually wired up
- links that lead to weak pages
- “dashboard sprawl”
- pages that should be panels inside another page instead of standalone destinations
- admin-only tools exposed too prominently
- pages that break trust because they feel unfinished
- pages that exist only because they were built, not because users need them

7. RECOMMENDED NAV REDESIGN
After the audit, propose a better nav structure.
Give me:
- the ideal top-level nav
- ideal sub-nav under each section
- renamed pages where needed
- pages to merge
- pages to cut
- pages to hide for pilot/demo
- pages that should become tabs, drawers, modals, or embedded panels instead of full pages

Design principle:
Fewer, stronger destinations.
Everything should feel obvious and intentional.

8. PRIORITIZED FIXES
Create a prioritized action list:
- Critical issues to fix immediately
- High-value workflow simplifications
- Pages to merge/remove
- Labeling / naming fixes
- Navigation cleanup
- Trust / polish fixes
- Optional future improvements

For each recommendation, include:
- Why it matters
- Who it affects
- How hard it is to fix
- Whether it is pilot-blocking, important, or nice-to-have

9. OUTPUT FORMAT
Use this exact structure:

A. Executive verdict
B. Current nav inventory
C. Section-by-section audit
D. Workflow audit
E. Biggest problems / messiest areas
F. Recommended nav redesign
G. Pages to cut / merge / hide
H. Top 20 highest-priority fixes
I. Final product cleanliness verdict

10. IMPORTANT RULES
- Be brutally honest.
- Do not just compliment the product.
- Do not just summarize features.
- Do not assume current structure is correct.
- If something is messy, say it clearly.
- If something should be cut, say cut it.
- If something should be merged, say exactly with what.
- If something is confusing for a BCBA, admin, or coordinator, explain why.
- Focus on real usability, workflow logic, and product clarity.
- Prefer simple, elegant, repeatable workflows over feature sprawl.

Final standard:
This should feel like a product design + UX + workflow + information architecture audit of the entire app, with the goal of making the whole nav cleaner, simpler, tighter, and more intuitive for daily use.
