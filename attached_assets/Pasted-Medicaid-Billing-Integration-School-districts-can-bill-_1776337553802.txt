Medicaid Billing Integration
School districts can bill Medicaid for SPED services (speech, OT, PT, counseling, transportation) but most districts recover only a fraction of what they're eligible for because the documentation is scattered. Trellis already captures session logs with student, provider, service type, and duration — that's 90% of what a Medicaid claim needs. Adding CPT code mapping, provider NPI tracking, and a claims export would let districts recover potentially hundreds of thousands of dollars per year. This is a feature districts will pay a premium for.

2. Compliance Risk Forecasting ("Cost Avoidance Dashboard")
Instead of just showing what's already out of compliance, predict what's about to go wrong: evaluations approaching the 30-day deadline, students trending toward missed service thresholds, IEP annual reviews within 30/60/90 days with no meeting scheduled. Frame this as "money you're about to lose" — each compliance failure has a quantifiable cost. Districts would pay extra for a module that shows: "You have 12 students at risk of compensatory obligations totaling an estimated $45,000."

3. Compensatory Services Tracker with Financial Impact
The app tracks missed services, but there's no view showing the dollar value of accumulated compensatory obligations. If a speech session costs $85/hour and a student is owed 14 hours, that's $1,190. Roll that up across all students and the district director can see the total financial exposure at a glance. This drives urgency and justifies the software's cost overnight.

Tier 2: Strong Revenue Features
4. IEP Meeting Efficiency Tools (Time = Money)
Every hour a SPED teacher spends in an IEP meeting is an hour they're not providing services — and often requires a sub. Features that shorten meetings: auto-generated draft agendas with pre-populated data, a "meeting prep" status showing what's ready and what's missing, and a one-click progress summary that eliminates flipping through binders. If this saves even 30 minutes per meeting across hundreds of annual reviews, the ROI is enormous.

5. Provider Utilization & Cost Analysis
Show districts exactly what each contracted provider is costing per student, per hour, compared to in-house staff. Highlight where agency reliance is highest and where hiring an FTE would save money. This is a feature that SPED directors would use to justify budget requests — making Trellis indispensable at renewal time.

6. Automated Progress Reports
Teachers spend 3–5 hours per student per quarter writing progress reports. If the app already has goal data and session logs, it can auto-generate narrative progress reports. Multiply the time savings across a district's caseload and you're talking about recovering hundreds of staff hours per reporting cycle.

Tier 3: Differentiation & Stickiness
7. Due Process Risk Scoring
Flag students where procedural safeguards are at risk: consent not obtained, timelines missed, required team members absent from meetings, parent concerns not documented. Each of these is a potential hearing trigger. A risk score per student helps directors prioritize where to invest attention before problems become lawsuits.

8. Out-of-District Placement ROI Tracker
For students in out-of-district placements, track whether goals are being met. If a district is paying $120K/year and the student isn't progressing, that's data to support transitioning them back in-district — saving the full placement cost.

9. Staff Workload & Burnout Indicators
SPED teacher turnover costs districts $15K–$25K per position in hiring and training. Track paperwork hours, caseload size, and overtime indicators. When workload crosses thresholds, flag it for admins. Retaining one teacher saves more than the software costs.
