Parent Communication Hub
Right now there's a guardian portal and communication logs, but no way to send messages, schedule parent conferences, or share progress updates directly through the app. A built-in messaging system with templates (e.g., "Prior Written Notice," meeting invitations) would save hours of email juggling.

2. Staff Scheduling & Availability
There's no calendar view showing which providers are available when, or a way to manage provider schedules across buildings. When a speech therapist is split between three schools, admins need to see their availability at a glance and resolve scheduling conflicts.

3. Reporting & Analytics Export
The dashboard shows compliance metrics, but there's no way to generate printable reports for school committee meetings or state reporting. Think: "Show me a PDF of all students out of compliance this quarter" or "Export a summary of services delivered by provider for the superintendent."

4. Document Management & Workflow
IEPs and evaluations exist, but there's no document version history with audit trail, no workflow for routing documents through an approval chain (draft → team review → director sign-off → parent), and no built-in Prior Written Notice generator.

5. Caseload Balancing Tool
Admins can see who's assigned where, but there's no tool to visualize caseload distribution across providers and flag imbalances — like one BCBA with 25 students while another has 8.

6. Substitute & Coverage Management
When a provider is absent, there's no way to quickly reassign their sessions for the day or track who covered what. This is a daily headache in most districts.

As a Special Education Teacher
1. Quick Session Timer
Logging sessions after the fact is error-prone. A built-in timer that lets me start/stop during a session and auto-fills the duration would be much more accurate and faster.

2. Progress Report Generator
There's goal data collection, but no tool that pulls all the data together into a formatted progress report I can attach to report cards. Right now I'd have to manually compile that from session logs.

3. Student Snapshot / At-a-Glance View
When I open a student, I want to instantly see: current goals and how they're trending, upcoming deadlines, recent session notes, and any behavior incidents — all on one screen instead of clicking through multiple tabs.

4. Goal Progress Visualization
The data is being collected, but there are no trend charts showing how a student is progressing toward each IEP goal over time. A simple line graph of "percent correct across sessions" would make data-driven decisions much easier.

5. Meeting Prep Checklist
Before an IEP meeting, I need to gather data, draft goals, contact parents, reserve a room, and invite the team. A guided checklist that tracks what's done and what's outstanding for each upcoming meeting would prevent things from falling through the cracks.

6. Collaboration Notes
There's no way for me and the OT, speech therapist, and para working with the same student to leave each other notes or flag concerns. A shared student-level comment thread would improve team coordination without needing separate email chains.

7. Accommodation Tracking
IEP accommodations are documented, but there's no way for general education teachers to quickly look up what accommodations a student needs, or for me to verify they're actually being implemented in mainstream classrooms.

Would you like me to prioritize any of these and start building them? I'd suggest starting with the ones that would have the biggest impact for demo readiness — the progress report generator, goal progress charts, and student snapshot view tend to be the features that really wow people in demos.
