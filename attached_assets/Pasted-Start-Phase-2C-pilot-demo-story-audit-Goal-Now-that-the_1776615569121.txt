Start Phase 2C: pilot/demo story audit.

Goal:
Now that the app is more coherent (Phase 2A) and the hands-on roles are more believable (Phase 2B), audit the product as a pilot/demo experience. Identify what should be emphasized, collapsed, hidden, or de-emphasized so the app tells a crisp story to a district buyer.

Core question:
If a district coordinator/admin saw this product in a 10-minute demo, what would feel essential, what would feel distracting, and what would feel confusing or too broad?

What I want from this audit:
1. Identify the true core pilot workflow/pages
2. Identify which pages/sections are helping the sales story
3. Identify which pages/sections are distracting, premature, duplicated, or noisy
4. Recommend a cleaner demo path and nav emphasis
5. Rank the next Phase 2C build tasks

Audit from the perspective of:
- district admin / coordinator buyer
- case-manager / teacher user
- BCBA / provider user

Focus on questions like:
- What should the demo open on?
- What are the 5–7 pages that actually matter in a pilot?
- Which nav sections/items make the app feel too sprawling?
- Which pages belong in the core wedge vs later expansion?
- What should be hidden, collapsed, or de-emphasized for demos?
- What exact 10-minute walkthrough would best sell this product?

Constraints:
- This is an audit and prioritization pass first, not a giant build
- No backend/schema/API changes
- No auth rewrite
- Be blunt and specific
- Prefer product clarity over feature maximalism

1. files/areas you plan to inspect
2. buyer/user perspectives you will evaluate
3. what counts as “core pilot” vs “noise”
4. biggest likely demo-story risks
5. what is out of scope

Then perform the audit and return:
