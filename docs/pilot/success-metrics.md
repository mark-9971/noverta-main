# Noverta Pilot Success Metrics

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** April 15, 2026

---

## What "The Pilot Worked" Means

A successful pilot means Noverta was actively used for real compliance workflows, reduced manual tracking effort, and gave the district confidence that 603 CMR 28.00 / 46.00 obligations are visible and manageable. The following five outcomes define success.

---

## Metric 1 — IEP Roster Coverage

**Target:** 100% of active IEPs entered into Noverta within 30 days of go-live.

**Why it matters:** If the roster isn't complete, every report and compliance alert is unreliable. This is the foundation metric.

**How to track:** Run the Active IEP Timeline CSV export (Reports → Exports) and compare the row count to the district's official IEP count. Verify counts match within ±2 (to allow for mid-period exits/entries).

---

## Metric 2 — Service Logging Adoption

**Target:** At least 80% of scheduled service sessions logged within 48 hours of the session date across all active case managers, for each full month of the pilot.

**Why it matters:** Service minutes compliance (603 CMR 28.09) depends on accurate session data. If sessions aren't logged, the system can't warn about mandate gaps.

**How to track:** Pull the Service Minutes CSV export at the end of each month. Compare "Sessions Completed" + "Missed Sessions" against expected sessions per mandate. A session completion+logging rate of ≥80% meets the threshold.

---

## Metric 3 — Incident Reporting Timeliness

**Target:** 100% of physical restraint and seclusion incidents logged in Noverta within 24 hours of the incident.

**Why it matters:** 603 CMR 46.04 requires verbal parent notification within 24 hours. Noverta tracks this; it only works if incidents are entered promptly.

**How to track:** Pull the Incidents CSV export monthly. For each row, verify that the logged date matches the incident date. Flag any incidents logged more than 24 hours after the incident date. Target: zero flagged incidents per month after the first 30 days.

---

## Metric 4 — Annual Review Compliance Visibility

**Target:** Zero IEPs expire without a 30-day advance alert being visible to the case manager in the system.

**Why it matters:** An overdue annual review is a direct IDEA/603 CMR violation. The alert system is useless if case managers don't see it.

**How to track:** At the end of the pilot, pull the Active IEP Timeline export and check for any IEPs where "Annual Review Status" = "overdue" that did not have a corresponding alert acknowledged in the system. Target: zero unacknowledged overdue reviews.

---

## Metric 5 — Staff Engagement

**Target:** Every case manager and coordinator logs in at least 3 times per week, averaged over the 90-day pilot.

**Why it matters:** A system nobody uses isn't a system. Engagement is the leading indicator that Noverta is replacing — not duplicating — existing workflows.

**How to track:** District admin reviews the staff roster page. Active case managers who are absent from session log activity for more than 5 consecutive school days will be flagged for check-in. Noverta can provide an aggregate login report on request.

---

## End-of-Pilot Review

At 90 days, Noverta will schedule a 30-minute review call with the district admin to walk through each metric. A written summary will be shared within 5 business days of that call. Metrics 1–4 must be fully met; Metric 5 must reach ≥80% of case managers to declare the pilot a success.
