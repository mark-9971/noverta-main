A. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
====================

Trellis has evolved correctly as a feature machine — the breadth of capability is
genuinely impressive for a SPED compliance product at this stage. The problem is
the interface has grown laterally with each feature without a corresponding
architectural consolidation pass. What exists is a developer-organized product,
not a user-organized one. The nav structure reflects the engineering taxonomy
(module-by-module), not the mental models of administrators, SPED teachers,
BCBAs, or paras who use it daily. The result: a highly capable platform that a
first-time admin will find intimidating, a daily user will find noisy, and a demo
prospect will find confusing before they even reach the core value.

The five most consequential structural problems:
  1. Compliance-related content is split across at least three separate sections
     of the nav.
  2. The admin nav has 40+ visible destinations before the first scroll.
  3. Student detail is a ~1,400-line mega-page that tries to render an entire
     record in one shot.
  4. Six distinct roles share two navs when they likely need four.
  5. The dashboard loads 9+ independent API calls to render its initial view.


B. WHAT THE APP CURRENTLY FEELS LIKE TO A USER
===============================================

First-time admin:
  Opens to a dashboard with a greeting, a compliance rate card, an action queue,
  a student risk list, a cost-exposure panel, evaluations section, meetings
  section, transitions section, and deadlines — all simultaneously. Before they
  have oriented, they are being asked to respond to five different urgency signals
  at once. The sidebar has seven collapsible sections, all open by default,
  scrolling far below the viewport.

Daily admin:
  The app becomes a tab-clicking exercise. "I need to check compliance" →
  /compliance → pick from 6 tabs. "I need a report" → /reports → pick from 9
  tabs. "I need to see sessions" → it is both a standalone page and embedded in
  Compliance. They learn their 3-4 routes and ignore everything else.

SPED teacher:
  Gets the full admin nav with label substitutions ("Directory" → "My
  Directory"). Sees ABA, Financial/Executive, and everything else an admin sees.
  Clutter with no clear entry point for their actual daily job. Their home route
  is / (admin dashboard), not /today, even though their nav lists Today as a
  primary item.

BCBA:
  Best-served of all staff roles — has a purpose-built nav. Still lands at two
  Overview items (Today + Dashboard) that serve similar purposes.

Para:
  Clean, simple nav. "My Day" is exactly right. But "Schedule" and "My Schedule"
  are two separate nav items. These should be one.

Guardian:
  Portal is clean and proportionate. 5 items, zero waste. The best-structured
  role experience in the product.


C. BIGGEST UX PROBLEMS
=======================

1. COMPLIANCE CONTENT IS SPLIT THREE WAYS
   /compliance has 6 tabs including a full Risk Report. /reports has 9 tabs
   including Compliance Trend, At-Risk, and Executive Summary. The dashboard
   itself surfaces compliance rate and a student risk list inline. A user trying
   to understand compliance status has to know which of these three surfaces to
   use, and when.

2. REPORTS TAB OVERLOAD
   9 tabs on a single page (executive, trend, audit, minutes, missed, risk,
   parent, exports, pilot-health) wrap on most screen sizes and have no clear
   hierarchy. Some (Audit Package, Pilot Health) belong in admin/settings. Some
   (Compliance Trend, At-Risk) belong merged into the Compliance section. The
   tab bar is unscannable.

3. SESSIONS DUPLICATION
   /sessions exists as a standalone nav item under Staffing AND as a tab inside
   /compliance. The exact same component is rendered in both places. A user
   encountering both does not know which is authoritative.

4. ADMIN NAV IS 40+ DESTINATIONS, ALL VISIBLE, MOST OPEN BY DEFAULT
   Overview (2), Compliance Tools (6 with sub-items), IEP (4 with sub-items),
   ABA (3), Staffing (3 with sub-items), Financial/Executive (10),
   Admin/Tools (3 with 8 sub-items). This is closer to a sitemap than a
   navigation system.

5. STUDENT DETAIL PAGE IS A MONOLITH
   At ~1,400 lines of render logic, it loads compliance, service requirements,
   IEP goals with charts, ABA behavior targets, program data, session history,
   medical contacts, emergency contacts, staff assignments, and compensatory
   exposure simultaneously. On a student with rich data this page is extremely
   slow to feel usable and visually overwhelming.

6. SCHEDULING HUB DOES NOT CONTAIN WHAT THE NAV SUGGESTS
   The hub renders 2 tabs (Weekly Schedule, Coverage). But the nav lists 4
   children (including Staff Calendar and Caseload Balancing). Users clicking
   "Staff Calendar" from the Scheduling Hub nav child land on /staff-calendar —
   a standalone page, not a hub tab. The parent/child relationship in the nav
   is a lie.

7. ADMIN, CASE_MANAGER, AND COORDINATOR SHARE ONE IDENTICAL NAV
   These roles have materially different daily workflows. A coordinator managing
   schedules and coverage has no reason to see Medicaid Billing, ABA tools, or
   Executive Dashboard. A case manager focused on IEPs does not need State
   Reporting or Compensatory Finance. One-nav-fits-all produces clutter for
   everyone.

8. ACCOMMODATION LOOKUP APPEARS IN TWO UNRELATED NAV SECTIONS
   Listed under Compliance Tools AND as a child of Transition Planning in the
   IEP section. The canonical location is unclear.

9. "TODAY" AND "MY DAY" ARE THE SAME CONCEPT WITH DIFFERENT NAMES
   /today (for BCBA/sped_teacher) and /my-day (for para) are functionally
   identical role-scoped daily views built as entirely separate page components
   with different routes, different names, and no shared code or mental model.

10. NO GLOBAL SEARCH / COMMAND PALETTE
    The app has /iep-search but no Cmd+K global search. Finding a student,
    provider, or session requires knowing which page surface to use first.

11. COMPENSATORY SERVICES AND COMPENSATORY FINANCE ARE SEPARATE NAV ITEMS
    FOR THE SAME DOMAIN
    One (Compliance Tools) tracks obligations. The other (Financial/Executive)
    tracks financial exposure. A user managing compensatory issues expects these
    in one place.

12. DASHBOARD FIRES 9 API CALLS ON INITIAL LOAD
    Compliance risk report, week trend, weekly summary, onboarding status,
    dashboard summary, accommodation compliance, evaluations dashboard,
    transitions dashboard, meetings dashboard. Heavy first paint. If any one
    fails, sections silently collapse or show skeletons with no explanation.

F. CORE WORKFLOW BREAKDOWNS
============================

1. ADMIN CHECKING COMPLIANCE STATUS (daily)

   Current path:
     Dashboard → compliance rate card → click "Review X students" →
     redirects to /compliance-risk-report → redirects again to
     /compliance#risk-report → loads tab → finds student list → clicks
     student → student detail page (slow, 10+ sections load).

   Friction:
     Two redirects. One full page load with 10 section renders before the
     user sees what they came for. The student detail page renders every
     section regardless of what the user navigated there for.

   Fix:
     Dashboard risk list should deep-link directly to the student detail
     compliance section. Eliminate the redirect chain. Student detail should
     open scoped to the Compliance tab.

2. ADMIN FOLLOWING UP ON A MISSED SESSION

   Current path:
     Dashboard action queue → "Follow up with X providers on Y missed
     sessions" → /sessions → user manually applies date + provider filters.

   Friction:
     /sessions opens completely unfiltered. The dashboard was flagging a
     specific provider/date, but the link drops the user into the full
     unfiltered session log with no pre-applied context.

   Fix:
     Dashboard provider action links should deep-link to
     /sessions?provider=X&status=missed so the filtered view is immediate.

3. SPED TEACHER STARTING THEIR DAY

   Current path:
     Sign in → lands on Dashboard (/) → compliance-focused, admin-oriented.
     Today's schedule, their students, and daily sessions are not visible.
     They must navigate to "Today" manually.

   Friction:
     sped_teacher.homeHref is "/" (admin dashboard). Their nav lists Today
     as a primary item. The home route contradicts the nav's intent.

   Fix:
     Set sped_teacher.homeHref to "/today". One-line change, major daily
     experience improvement.

4. WRITING AN IEP DRAFT

   Current path:
     IEP section → IEP Hub (/iep) → Builder sub-item → IEP Builder
     landing → select student → multi-step wizard.

   Friction:
     Two hub layers before reaching the actual builder. /iep.tsx is a
     landing page that shows 4 cards pointing to pages already accessible
     from the sidebar. It adds a click tax with no informational benefit
     for returning users.

   Fix:
     Make IEP Builder a direct first-class nav item. Remove the hub landing
     for power users (it can remain as an onboarding aid for new users via
     a tooltip/quickstart overlay).

5. PARA RUNNING THEIR DAILY SCHEDULE

   Current path:
     Sign in → My Day (/my-day). This is good. The para workflow is the
     cleanest in the app.

   Problem:
     Para nav has "Schedule" (/schedule) and "My Schedule" (/my-schedule)
     as two separate items. These should be one destination.

   Fix:
     Merge into a single "My Schedule" item pointing to /my-schedule.

6. ADMIN RUNNING A COMPLIANCE REPORT FOR A MEETING

   Current path:
     Reports → pick from 9 tabs → find the right report → generate/export.

   Friction:
     "Audit Package," "Pilot Health," "Exports" require knowing what they
     are before the user can find them. No descriptions on the tab bar. 9
     tabs are not scannable. First 3 tabs have icons, last 6 do not.

   Fix:
     Reports page needs a landing view that describes each report type
     with a one-sentence description before the user commits to a tab.
     Reduce to 4-5 tabs maximum.

7. LOGGING A SESSION (any provider)

   Current path:
     Sessions page → "+ Log Session" → modal → fill in student, service
     type, date, duration → save.

   Friction:
     Session logging is not accessible from the student detail page in a
     single click. There is no "quick log" entry point from student-adjacent
     surfaces. The floating timer handles active sessions but not
     retrospective entries. Admins reviewing session logs cannot quickly
     log a corrective entry from the same surface the timer occupies.

8. GUARDIAN CHECKING ON THEIR CHILD

   Path: Guardian portal → clean 5-item nav. This is the best-structured
   role experience in the entire product. No changes needed.


G. PAGE-BY-PAGE RECOMMENDATIONS
=================================

DASHBOARD (/)
  Good:
    Compliance rate hero, trend arrows (week-over-week deltas), urgency-tiered
    action queue, student risk list with mini progress bars.
  Confusing:
    9 API calls on load. The secondary sections (Evaluations, Transitions,
    Meetings, Deadlines) are always collapsed on first render — why load and
    render them if collapsed? No "as of" timestamp on any data card, so the
    admin cannot tell if they're looking at today's data or yesterday's.
  Overloaded:
    Simultaneously a monitoring screen (daily use), an alert surface (urgent
    action), and an onboarding wizard (setup). Three different jobs.
  Missing:
    A "last updated" timestamp. A way to act directly from the student risk
    list without navigating to another page. An "as of" date on the
    compliance rate card.
  Direction:
    Two modes: "Setup mode" (full-screen checklist when onboarding incomplete)
    and "Daily ops mode" (compliance rate + action queue + student risk list
    only). Defer secondary sections behind lazy load or a separate Status
    Digest page.

COMPLIANCE (/compliance)
  Good:
    6-tab structure is internally coherent. Risk Report tab is genuinely
    useful. Compliance rate bar chart per service type is informative.
  Confusing:
    Sessions tab renders the exact same Sessions page component as /sessions.
    Users navigating from Staffing > Sessions vs. Compliance > Sessions tab
    see identical UI with no indication they are in a different context.
  Overloaded:
    Tries to be: data entry surface (sessions), analytical surface (risk
    report, trends), and checklist tracker simultaneously.
  Missing:
    A hero/summary card at the top of the page. New users have no orientation
    context — the page starts immediately with a tab bar.
  Direction:
    Remove Sessions tab entirely (belongs in Staffing). Compliance becomes:
    Service Minutes, Checklist, Timeline, Trends. Risk Report becomes its own
    nav item or merges into Reports.

STUDENT DETAIL (/students/:id)
  Good:
    All student data in one place is the right intent. Progress rings, session
    history, goal charts with annotations, compensatory summary are genuinely
    valuable.
  Confusing:
    Renders 7+ distinct sections simultaneously. On first load this is a
    wall of content with no clear reading order and no section navigation.
  Overloaded:
    Severely. The most overloaded page in the product.
  Missing:
    Section anchor navigation. A "fold" indicator showing what's below the
    visible area. A way to print or export a student summary that is
    discoverable (Share Progress button is not obvious).
  Direction:
    Convert to tabbed student record:
      Summary (compliance ring + key facts + service requirements)
      IEP & Goals
      Behavior / ABA
      Sessions
      Contacts & Medical
    Reduces first-paint data requirement by approximately 80%.

ALERTS (/alerts)
  Good:
    Tabbed (Open/Snoozed/Resolved). Alert badge in nav is useful.
  Confusing:
    Alerts and the dashboard action queue represent two different
    prioritization systems a user must mentally reconcile. System-generated
    alerts vs. dashboard-computed actions are different things presented as
    parallel surfaces.
  Direction:
    Unify conceptually. Dashboard action queue surfaces top-priority alerts.
    /alerts is the full alert management surface. Link from action queue items
    to their corresponding alerts.

REPORTS (/reports)
  Good:
    Breadth of report types. Audit Package with print/download is valuable.
    Scheduled report exports are a strong differentiator.
  Confusing:
    9 tabs. First 3 have icons, last 6 do not — inconsistent. Terse labels
    ("Minutes," "Missed," "Risk") are not self-explanatory to new users.
    Mixes operational reports, strategic reports, workflow tools, and
    admin-only tools in the same tab bar with no hierarchy.
  Direction:
    Landing view that categorizes reports: Compliance Reports, Operational
    Reports, Export Tools, Admin-Only. Maximum 4-5 tabs.

IEP HUB (/iep)
  Good:
    Exists as an orientation surface for new users.
  Confusing:
    It is a landing page showing 4 cards (Search, Builder, Meetings, Calendar)
    already reachable from the sidebar. Net effect: adds one mandatory click
    to every IEP workflow. For returning users it is pure overhead.
  Direction:
    Remove the hub wrapper for navigational purposes. Keep IEP-related pages
    as direct nav items. The hub can live as an onboarding tour stop.

SCHEDULING HUB (/scheduling)
  Good:
    Concept of a scheduling-focused hub is correct.
  Confusing:
    Hub renders 2 tabs (Weekly Schedule, Coverage). Nav lists 4 children
    (adding Staff Calendar and Caseload Balancing). Clicking those nav
    children opens standalone pages, not hub tabs. The hierarchy is
    misleading.
  Direction:
    Either make the hub render all 4 tabs, or remove the hub wrapper and
    use flat nav items in the Staffing section.

SETTINGS (/settings)
  Good:
    Grouped in a single place.
  Confusing:
    8 tabs mix true configuration (General, School Year, Billing Rates,
    SIS Integration) with investigative/diagnostic tools (Audit Log,
    System Status, Recently Deleted) and legal documents (Legal &
    Compliance).
  Direction:
    Split into Settings (configuration only) and Admin Tools (Audit Log,
    System Status, Recently Deleted, Data Health). Admin Tools becomes its
    own nav section separate from configuration.

PARA MY DAY (/my-day)
  Good:
    Clean, purpose-built, actionable. The right model for role-specific screens.
    Daily session agenda, coverage context, program data entry in one surface.
  Problem:
    Para nav has both "Schedule" (/schedule) and "My Schedule" (/my-schedule)
    as separate items. One of these should be removed.
  Direction:
    Keep /my-schedule. Remove the generic /schedule link from the para nav.

SESSIONS (/sessions)
  Good:
    Filterable table, session logging modal.
  Confusing:
    Three entry points: Staffing > Sessions, Compliance > Sessions tab, Para
    nav > Session Log. All three render identical UI. No canonical home.
  Direction:
    Sessions lives once, in Staffing. Remove the Sessions tab from Compliance.
    The Para nav link becomes the single path for paras.
